Profile

I was born and grew up in Pasco, Washington with my brother Tanner Savage and my parents Tom and Salina. It is a small town in South Eastern Washington and is part of a greater region called the Tri-Cities. This region is commonly known for creating the plutonium for the Manhattan Project and has a diverse population ranging from farmers to nuclear physicists. My parents created their own business called Savage Logistics transporting hazardous materials such as nuclear waste for the various projects around the area.

I went to UCSD after high school and declared my major in chemical engineering but because of my interest in programming after my third year began to explore jobs in that field. I have learned many languages such as C++, C#, HTML, Java, and MatLab and have an interest in learning many more.

During the summer of 2013 I worked at SAIC programming a windows program that aided the installation of cameras for Radiation Portal Monitors. These cameras were used to record license plate numbers and digitally link them with radiation scans of and particular vehicle.

In the winter of 2014-2015 I worked with SOITEC helping improve their MES controller for their automated solar assembly systems. I performed basic IT work as well as provided support for the automated systems and tracked root causes for system failures to ensure the factory could run for extended periods of time.
